Simple\+Bar is a plugin that tries to solve a long time problem\+: how to get custom scrollbars for your web-\/app while keeping a good user experience? Simple\+Bar {\bfseries{does NOT implement a custom scroll behaviour}}. It keeps the {\bfseries{native}} {\ttfamily overflow\+: auto} scroll and {\bfseries{only}} replace the scrollbar visual appearance.

Simple\+Bar is meant to be as easy to use as possible and lightweight. If you want something more advanced I recommend \href{https://github.com/KingSora}{\texttt{ King\+Sora}} \textquotesingle{}s \href{https://kingsora.github.io/OverlayScrollbars/}{\texttt{ Overlay Scrollbars}}.\hypertarget{md_public_2_muse_2assets_2vendor_2simplebar_2_r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md226}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Installation}{Installation}}\label{md_public_2_muse_2assets_2vendor_2simplebar_2_r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md226}

\href{https://grsmto.github.io/simplebar/}{\texttt{ https\+://grsmto.\+github.\+io/simplebar/}}\hypertarget{md_public_2_muse_2assets_2vendor_2simplebar_2_r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md256}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{4. How it works}{4. How it works}}\label{md_public_2_muse_2assets_2vendor_2simplebar_2_r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md256}
Simple\+Bar only does one thing\+: replace the browser\textquotesingle{}s default scrollbars with a custom CSS-\/styled scrollbar without sacrificing performance. Unlike most other plugins, Simple\+Bar doesn\textquotesingle{}t mimic scroll behaviour with Javascript, which typically causes jank and strange scrolling behaviour. You keep the awesomeness of native scrolling… with a custom scrollbar! Design your scrollbar how you like, with CSS, across all browsers.

For the most part Simple\+Bar uses the browser\textquotesingle{}s native scrolling functionality, but replaces the conventional scrollbar with a custom CSS-\/styled scrollbar. The plugin listens for scroll events and redraws the custom scrollbar accordingly.

Key to this technique is hiding the native browser scrollbar. The scrollable element is made slightly wider/taller than its containing element, effectively hiding the scrollbar from view.\hypertarget{md_public_2_muse_2assets_2vendor_2simplebar_2_r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md257}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{5. Caveats}{5. Caveats}}\label{md_public_2_muse_2assets_2vendor_2simplebar_2_r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md257}

\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Simple\+Bar can\textquotesingle{}t be used on the {\ttfamily \texorpdfstring{$<$}{<}body\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}}, {\ttfamily \texorpdfstring{$<$}{<}textarea\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}}, {\ttfamily \texorpdfstring{$<$}{<}table\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}} or {\ttfamily \texorpdfstring{$<$}{<}select\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}} elements. {\ttfamily \texorpdfstring{$<$}{<}iframe\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}} should be working (depends of your usecase). If you are looking to support these, I suggest taking a look at \href{https://kingsora.github.io/OverlayScrollbars}{\texttt{ Over\+Lay\+Scrollbars}}.
\item Simple\+Bar doesn\textquotesingle{}t currently support {\ttfamily overflow\+: visible}. Which means any children of your scrolling div will be clipped (like with {\ttfamily overflow\+: hidden}).
\end{DoxyItemize}
